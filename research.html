Research


          

Endomicroscope

I have designed and developed several variations of novel imaging instruments and systems, including advanced endoscope systems such as wide-field endoscopes, dual-axis confocal hyperspectral endoscopes, and photoacoustic endoscopes.  The foundation of these systems lies in a MEMS-mirror scanner-oriented design.

Throughout the development process, I have managed various stages: optical system design utilizing ray-tracing, mechanical packaging design with CAD, system integration using principles of mechatronics, as well as control strategies for real-time image reconstruction and display through DAQ.

Each type of endoscope I developed has unique capabilities: The wide-field endoscopes I designed have the ability to execute multiplexed fluorescence collection with an extensive field-of-view.  The dual-axis confocal hyperspectral endoscope that I have developed possesses the capability to offer sub-cellular resolution and depth, making it suitable for point-of-care diagnoses or optical biopsies.  Moreover, the photoacoustic endoscopes that I have worked on facilitate deep tissue penetration, making them pivotal tools for cancer staging.

For more in-depth information about each project and a deeper exploration of their functionalities and benefits, please refer to the publications section.  

Clinical Trials

Several clinical trials, including NCT04304781, NCT03852576, NCT03220711, and NCT03589443 as listed on ClinicalTrials.gov, have been conducted. In these trials, we showcased innovative imaging methodologies that incorporated the use of targeted contrast agents along with the novel imaging instruments we've developed.

In addition to this, we utilized the amassed imaging data to develop an effective deep learning model. This model was applied to classify the stage of the disease, further enhancing our understanding and diagnosis capabilities.

These trials have been pivotal in both validating and elevating the capabilities of our imaging systems within a clinical setting. For a more comprehensive understanding and detailed information regarding these trials, please refer to the publications section and ClinicalTrials.gov.
